COMMUNICATION AND BUSINESS TODAY

In in our everyday life some things are inevitable,one of such is communication, this is because they are characterized or are paramount to our day to day activities. This is also applicable to the business world, it’s absence or poor application can lead to poor results.

Communication which is derived from the Latin word commūnicāre meaning “to share”, has been given various definitions, examples are; “the exchange of information between persons”, as stated in Marriam Webster Dictionary. According Business Dictionary; it’s a two-way process of reaching mutual understanding, in which participants not only exchange (encode-decode) information, news, ideas and feelings but also create and share meanings. In general, communication is a means of connecting people or places. In business, it is a key function of management–an organization cannot operate without communication between levels, departments and employees.

It’s very important that Employers, Employees, Entrepreneurs, C.E.O’s, Organizations, Businesses, Customers and Individuals work on effective communication skills, because it does leave a gap that is very visible in terms of relationship, understanding, persuasion and loyalty if not well tackled. Good communication is a tool to project good image, about an organization or business, it goes a long way to help speak for and to maintain this image. That is to say it is inevitable for Public Relation purposes.

I have been to organisations where good communications is the most important tool to sell their products and services and yet they mess around with this opportunities, examples of such organisation are banks in Nigeria, and the result leads to depreciation in the market. This problems shoul be combated, organisations and businesses irrespective of the size should make sure they brief their employees on communication skills, it drive sells and facilitates customer loyalty.

Most of the problems of businesses today isn’t the product, rather inability to communicate and sell the image of their organisation to the public and anticipated customers. And it does affect their advertising strength and market penetration. The purpose of this write up is to urge businesses to understand the role communication in selling their products, building good customers service orientations and customer relationship that helps in persuading and creating customer loyalty.

Proper orientation about this subject will enlighten our society and improve businesses within its environs, it should be an all go area for organisations to ensure that their staffs have a proper knowledge of it.

Being a good communicator will help you loose nothing, but lacking in it can cost you a lot.
